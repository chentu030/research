# 3041 揚智 深度研究報告

## 一句話摘要

揚智 (3041) 正從傳統數位機上盒 (STB) 晶片供應商轉型為 AIoT 與 ASIC 設計服務雙引擎成長策略公司。儘管2025年仍處虧損，但在新產品出貨與ASIC業務推動下，毛利率顯著改善，目標2025年下半年單季轉盈，並透過多項策略聯盟，佈局AIoT與企業資安，有望在2026年及其後迎來營收與獲利成長的反轉契機。

## 公司概覽

揚智科技主要業務為數位機上盒（STB）晶片設計，近年來積極轉型，將特殊應用晶片（ASIC）設計服務與矽智財（IP）授權作為其「雙引擎」成長策略。

**核心產品與服務：**

*   **數位機上盒（STB）晶片：** 全球前三大STB晶片供應商之一，服務全球超過五億家庭用戶，產品通過高安全標準認證，滿足運營商系統整合需求。多媒體串流系統級晶片支援4K視訊、高速乙太網路並具備全球最低功耗。
*   **ASIC設計服務與IP矽智財：** 利用既有IP基礎（處理器運算、傳輸、影音等），佈局40奈米、28/22奈米製程，與台積電(2330)和聯電(2303)等晶圓廠合作。預計2025年將貢獻客戶委託設計（NRE）及矽智財（IP）授權的收入。
*   **AIoT系統晶片設計：** 規劃跨入AI系統晶片設計領域，開發具備影音互動、高階音頻與觸控應用的系統級晶片，應用於無人機、掃地機器人、離線GPT等高階市場，以及工業控制器、家電控制音箱等邊緣AI應用；低功耗產品則涵蓋AI攝影機、人臉辨識器、穿戴設備、個人醫療儀器等。

**營收結構：**

| :------------ | :-------------------- | :----------------------------------------- |
| 數位機上盒晶片 | ~90%                  | 傳統核心業務，在新興市場仍有成長空間。     |
| ASIC / IP     | 顯著成長中            | 2025年預計顯著貢獻NRE及IP授權收入，成長動能。 |
| AIoT 系統晶片  | 發展初期              | 戰略佈局，預期未來貢獻營收。               |

**製造基地：**
揚智為無晶圓廠 (Fabless) IC 設計公司，不擁有製造廠房，晶圓主要委由聯電（2303）代工，並與台積電（2330）合作。

**所屬細分產業與相關題材：**
「電子上游-IC設計」，半導體業。相關題材包括：STB、ASIC、IP、AIoT、邊緣AI、6G網路技術、新興市場（印度、巴西、非洲等）。

## 核心競爭優勢

1.  **STB 市場領先地位與深厚技術積累：** 作為全球前三大STB晶片供應商，服務全球逾五億家庭用戶，產品通過高安全標準認證，具備滿足運營商系統整合的專業能力。新一代DVB-S2X/DVB-C SoC F6P已於2024年量產，鞏固在新興市場的領先地位。
2.  **ASIC 設計服務與 IP 矽智財策略轉型：** 積極利用既有IP基礎，佈局40奈米及28/22奈米製程的ASIC設計服務與IP授權，有效拓展客製化晶片市場，降低對單一產品線的依賴，並提升產品附加價值。
3.  **前瞻佈局 AIoT 與邊緣 AI 晶片：** 規劃跨入AI系統晶片設計領域，開發具備影音互動、高階音頻與觸控應用的SoC，應用於無人機、掃地機器人、離線GPT及工業/家電控制等廣泛邊緣AI應用，與市場趨勢高度契合。
4.  **穩健的新興市場策略：** 長期專注印度、巴西、非洲等新興市場，有效避開美國市場的貿易風險，並成功取得大型標案，確保STB業務的穩定出貨量。
5.  **策略性合作夥伴網絡：** 獲得大宇資訊(6111)策略投資，並與光禾感知科技(AI智能客服)、ACCESS NetRange(雲端瀏覽器)以及東南亞電信運營商(AIoT系統晶片)建立合作關係，形成多元且強大的生態系。

### 產業趨勢與對揚智的影響

1.  **AI 晶片與邊緣 AI 快速發展：**
    *   **趨勢：** AI晶片市場2024年銷售額逾**500億美元**，預計2027年可破**4,000億美元**。邊緣AI需求激增，NPU技術創新提升AI SoC性能與能效。
    *   **機會：** 揚智規劃跨入AI系統晶片設計，開發具備影音互動及高階音頻與觸控應用的系統級晶片，並鎖定無人機、掃地機器人、離線GPT等市場，直接切入AIoT與邊緣AI的藍海市場，潛力巨大。
2.  **高解析度影音內容與串流服務普及：**
    *   **趨勢：** 4K/8K高畫質內容和OTT串流服務需求增長，推動對STB晶片處理能力和網路連結功能的需求。
    *   **機會：** 揚智在全球STB晶片市場的地位和多媒體串流SoC的專業能力，使其能受益於此趨勢，提供更高性能的機上盒晶片。
3.  **ASIC 設計服務與先進製程重要性提升：**
    *   **趨勢：** 客製化晶片需求因AI、汽車、5G等行業激增，ASIC設計服務市場成長快速。
    *   **機會：** 揚智的ASIC設計服務與IP授權策略，使其能為特定應用提供差異化晶片解決方案，提升產品附加價值，並降低對單一市場的依賴。

**威脅：**
*   **傳統STB市場競爭加劇及技術迭代：** OTT串流服務的興起對傳統STB市場構成挑戰，揚智需持續創新。
*   **半導體供需波動與地緣政治風險：** 儘管市場復甦，庫存與需求波動、地緣政治不確定性仍可能影響晶圓供應和成本。
*   **大型競爭對手壓力：** 在ASIC設計服務和AI SoC領域，將面臨國際大廠的激烈競爭。

**相關投資題材連結：**
*   **AI (人工智慧)：** 揚智明確投入AI系統晶片設計 (無人機、掃地機器人、離線GPT等)，直接受益於AI市場成長。
*   **HBM (高頻寬記憶體)：** 揚智雖不生產HBM，但AI驅動HBM需求激增，間接推動對高效能晶片與先進製程的需求，有利於揚智在ASIC及AI SoC領域的發展。
*   **電動車 (EV)：** 揚智目前無直接涉入，但其ASIC設計經驗與AI佈局，未來可能為其拓展至汽車電子ASIC市場提供潛在機會。

                                                                                                                                             The global micro-economy is at a critical juncture, with key indicators showing mixed signals that warrant careful monitoring. Recent data highlights the increasing complexity of the global economic landscape, driven by evolving geopolitical dynamics, technological advancements, and shifting consumer behaviors.

### **Global Economic Growth Outlook**

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) projects global growth to slow from **3.5% in 2022** to **3.0% in 2023 and 2024**. This modest deceleration reflects a confluence of factors, including persistent inflation, tighter monetary policies, and ongoing geopolitical tensions.

*   **Advanced Economies:** Growth in advanced economies is expected to decline from **2.7% in 2022** to **1.5% in 2023** and **1.4% in 2024**. This slowdown is largely attributed to aggressive interest rate hikes aimed at curbing inflation. The Eurozone, for instance, faces particular headwinds from elevated energy prices and manufacturing sector weakness.
*   **Emerging Market and Developing Economies (EMDEs):** EMDEs are projected to see a slight dip from **4.1% in 2022** to **4.0% in 2023**, before modestly recovering to **4.1% in 2024**. This resilience is partly due to the reopening boost in China and robust domestic demand in several Asian economies. However, many low-income countries continue to grapple with high debt levels and food insecurity.

### **Inflationary Pressures and Monetary Policy**

Global inflation remains elevated, although showing signs of moderation. The IMF forecasts global inflation to fall from **8.7% in 2022** to **6.8% in 2023** and **5.2% in 2024**. This downward trend is supported by easing supply chain bottlenecks and the cumulative effect of monetary tightening.

*   **Core Inflation:** Despite headline inflation declines, core inflation (excluding volatile food and energy prices) remains sticky, particularly in advanced economies. This suggests underlying demand pressures and tight labor markets are still at play. For example, in the Eurozone, core inflation stood at **5.5% in July 2023**, significantly above the European Central Bank's 2% target.
*   **Central Bank Responses:** Major central banks, including the Federal Reserve, European Central Bank, and Bank of England, have continued their hawkish stance. The **U.S. Federal Reserve** raised its benchmark interest rate by a cumulative **525 basis points** since March 2022, reaching a target range of **5.25%-5.50%** in July 2023. Similar aggressive tightening has been observed globally, with the aim of restoring price stability.

### **Labor Markets**

Labor markets in many advanced economies remain remarkably tight, contributing to wage growth and inflationary pressures.

*   **Unemployment Rates:** The **U.S. unemployment rate** registered **3.5% in July 2023**, near a 50-year low. Similarly, the **Eurozone unemployment rate** was **6.4% in June 2023**, an all-time low for the bloc.
*   **Wage Growth:** Strong demand for labor has translated into robust wage growth. In the U.S., **average hourly earnings increased by 4.4% year-on-year in July 2023**. While beneficial for workers, persistent high wage growth poses a challenge to central banks attempting to bring inflation down to target levels without triggering a sharp economic downturn.

### **Trade and Global Supply Chains**

Global trade volumes are experiencing a deceleration after the post-pandemic rebound. The World Trade Organization (WTO) anticipates global merchandise trade volume growth to slow to **1.7% in 2023**, down from **2.7% in 2022**.

*   **Regionalization Trends:** Geopolitical tensions and the desire for supply chain resilience are driving trends towards regionalization and "friendshoring." This could lead to higher production costs in the long run but enhance security of supply.
*   **Energy Prices:** While global energy prices have retreated from their 2022 peaks, they remain volatile. **Brent crude oil prices**, for instance, fluctuated between **$75 and $85 per barrel** in July 2023, influenced by supply cuts from OPEC+ and demand concerns.

### **Financial Market Developments**

Financial conditions have tightened significantly in response to monetary policy actions, yet equity markets have shown resilience in certain sectors.

*   **Bond Yields:** Government bond yields have surged. The **U.S. 10-year Treasury yield** reached over **4.0%** in August 2023, reflecting expectations of higher-for-longer interest rates.
*   **Equity Markets:** Despite tighter financial conditions, major equity indices, particularly in the U.S., have performed well in the first half of 2023. The **S&P 500 index rose by approximately 19% year-to-date by August 2023**, largely driven by optimism around artificial intelligence and robust earnings from tech giants. However, this masks underlying vulnerabilities in other sectors more sensitive to interest rate hikes.

### **Key Risks to the Outlook**

1.  **Inflation Persistence:** A resurgence or persistent stickiness of inflation, especially core inflation, could force central banks to tighten even further, increasing the risk of a harder landing.
2.  **Geopolitical Fragmentation:** Escalating geopolitical tensions (e.g., in Ukraine, U.S.-China relations) could disrupt energy and food markets, reignite supply chain pressures, and dampen investment.
3.  **Financial Stability Risks:** Rapid and aggressive monetary tightening could expose fragilities in the financial system, particularly in sectors with high leverage or exposure to rising interest rates (e.g., commercial real estate).
4.  **China's Economic Slowdown:** A more pronounced slowdown in China, beyond current projections, could have significant ripple effects on global trade and commodity markets, given its role as a major growth engine. China's **Q2 2023 GDP growth** of **6.3% year-on-year** was below expectations, highlighting challenges in its post-reopening recovery.

### **Conclusion**

The global micro-economy is navigating a challenging transition phase. While inflation shows signs of easing, underlying pressures remain, necessitating continued vigilance from central banks. Labor markets are resilient, but this contributes to inflationary concerns. The divergence in growth prospects between advanced economies and resilient emerging markets, coupled with rising geopolitical uncertainties, paints a complex picture. Investors and policymakers alike must carefully monitor these evolving dynamics to mitigate risks and capitalize on emerging opportunities in a fragmented and uncertain global landscape.
